3) Responsive Coding

Responsive coding might sound a bit tough, but you shouldn't get scared, it's actually really easy. What is important to keep in mind is that there are 3 layouts, one for desktop, one for landscape smartphone views and one for portrait smartphone views.

It's done through media queries, and in this case we use two. One with a max-width of 460px and one with a max-width of 320px.

3) Main Image for Mobile View

Thanks to the responsiveness of this email, we can now target our clients better. Most of the clients who are looking at the template on a phone don't really see anything, especially when it comes to the hero banner of your email. So the banner which would normally be designed to attract the most attention will pass unnoticed, that's why we need to change it for mobile views and put a new one designed especially for mobile purposes.

That's why in the stylesheet we change the "main_image" background using this code:

        	td[class="main_image"] {
		width: 440px !important;
		height: 212px !important;
		background: url('http://www.am-themes.com/wd_email/http://www.am-themes.com/wd_email/images_doc/wd-email2_06_mb.png') no-repeat;
		display: block;
		background-size: contain;
		}

3) Mailchimp Support

Please, follow these steps to import any Template to MailChimp:

1. Go to http://www.mailchimp.com and login.
2. Simply go to My Templates.



3. Click 'Import Template' and choose your this Template from the desktop.



4. Simply click on upload and you're good to go!

Here is a clear explanation of how to import a template: http://kb.mailchimp.com/article/how-do-i-import-a-template/
